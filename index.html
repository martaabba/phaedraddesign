Professional and Creative Resume

What is your design process?

I usually start with looking at the big picture. What do we want to accomplish? What are some of the obstacles that we can already see? What are the assets that we have at our disposal? I also think in color pallettes first, page layout starts to form, and then the flow of the site comes together. I am a collaborative designer who looks for feedback periodically, but can make decisions when they need to be made.

Why did you decide to become a web-designer?

After working in the banking industry for more than 8 years as a business banker, I needed to take stock and really re-assess what I wanted to spend eight plus hours a day doing with my life. It wasn’t banking. What I realized is that I really loved working with my business owners to help them with solutions that would work specifically for them. I liked being on their team. I was also involved in the Portland theatre scene as a designer and director. When my daughter was born, I had to choose between being home at night after work with her or in a theatre. I chose her, but I need a creative outlet. This profession is a perfect match for my temperament and my need to tinker creatively.

Where do you go for inspiration?

Portland, Oregon is a pretty inspiring place. Living so close to the ocean, the mountains, the river, and the desert allows for a lot of opportunities to get inspired. But so does the city. I love street art, graffiti, guerilla theatre, skate parks, dog parks, theatres, amusement parks, Forest Park, music venues, food trucks…getting inspired, for me, is about getting out in the world and seeing it. Then I can distill it into something to share.

Do you do graphic design?

I do not, however, I know several graphic design firms that would be happy to take a look at your needs. I can provide those referrals upon request.
